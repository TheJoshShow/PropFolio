---

## 1. Problem

- **Who:** Individual real estate investors and small operators evaluating rental properties.
- **Pain:** Hard to quickly assess “Should I buy this property?” without spreadsheets, multiple tools, and inconsistent data. Confidence in numbers (rent, expenses, deal quality) is low; decision-making is slow and fragmented.
- **Insight:** A single, score-first mobile experience that focuses on the core question and builds confidence in the numbers (with clear data limitations) can win early adopters and expand over time.

---

## 2. Solution

- **Product:** PropFolio — iOS-first app that helps users add properties (address or link), save them to a portfolio, and (roadmap) see confidence and deal scores so they can decide faster.
- **Core question:** “Should I buy this property?”
- **Differentiation:** Score-first, signal-driven UI; subscription model; no MLS dependency in MVP; clear “informational only” positioning to avoid regulatory overreach.
- **Stage:** [Pre-launch / launched / post–App Store with X users.]

## 3. Business Model

- **Revenue:** Subscription (Pro) — unlimited property imports and (roadmap) scoring and analysis. Free tier: 2 imports to drive trial.
- **Platform:** iOS App Store; in-app purchase via RevenueCat; no paid APIs called directly from client (backend/Edge Functions for third-party data).
- **Unit economics:** [LTV, CAC, payback — fill when available.] Focus on: conversion from free to Pro, retention, and low cost to serve.

## 4. Market

- **TAM/SAM/SOM:** [Customize.] E.g., U.S. single-family and small multifamily investors; number of deals per year; willingness to pay for tools.
- **Competition:** Spreadsheets, generic CRMs, other proptech tools. PropFolio competes on focus (one question), mobile-first, and clarity of scoring (confidence + deal score as we ship them).
- **Regulation:** Positioned as informational only; not investment, tax, or legal advice. No MLS dependency in MVP reduces licensing risk.
