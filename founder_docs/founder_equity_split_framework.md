# PropFolio — Founder Equity Split Framework

**Purpose:** Principles and options for splitting founder equity; supports discussion and eventual formal agreements.  
**Audience:** Founders; counsel when drafting stock/option documents  
**Date:** 2025-03-12

---

## 1. Disclaimer

**[Attorney review recommended.]** Equity splits have tax, securities, and governance implications. This document is a **framework for discussion** only. All actual grants, vesting, and rights must be documented in instruments (e.g., stock purchase agreements, option grants, bylaws) prepared or reviewed by qualified U.S. counsel.

---

## 2. Principles

- **Clarity:** Splits should be agreed and documented in writing before or at formation.
- **Vesting:** Founder equity should be subject to vesting (see [vesting_schedule_framework.md](vesting_schedule_framework.md)).
- **Fairness:** Consider past contribution, future role, risk, and time commitment.
- **Simplicity:** Avoid overly complex formulas unless there is a clear reason (e.g., milestone-based grants).

---

## 3. Common Approaches

| Approach | Description | Pros | Cons |
|----------|-------------|------|------|
| **Equal split** | 50/50 or 33/33/33 | Simple; signals equal commitment | May not reflect unequal contribution or role |
| **Role-based** | Weight by title (e.g., CEO 40%, CTO 40%, COO 20%) | Aligns with responsibility | Can feel arbitrary without clear criteria |
| **Contribution-based** | Weight by past/future contribution (idea, IP, capital, full-time vs. part-time) | Can feel fair if criteria are clear | Hard to quantify; can cause disagreement |
| **Hybrid** | Base equal + adjustments for capital, IP, or role | Flexible | More complex to document |

---

## 4. Factors to Consider

- **Idea / concept:** Who originated the idea? (Often not given majority weight alone.)
- **IP / code:** Who contributed pre-formation IP assigned to the company?
- **Capital:** Who invested cash or forgone salary? Document and consider as separate from “sweat” equity.
- **Full-time vs. part-time:** Full-time founders often receive larger share than part-time or advisory roles.
- **Future role:** CEO vs. CTO vs. other; alignment with [founder_roles_and_responsibilities.md](founder_roles_and_responsibilities.md).
- **Risk:** Who joined earlier, left other employment, or took more personal risk?

---

## 5. Documentation

- **Cap table:** Record all founder shares in [cap_table_template.csv](cap_table_template.csv).
- **Instruments:** Founder shares should be issued pursuant to:
  - Stock purchase agreement (or equivalent) with vesting and repurchase/restriction terms
  - IP assignment (see [ip_assignment_agreement_draft.md](ip_assignment_agreement_draft.md))
  - Compliance with applicable securities laws (e.g., 83(b) election if applicable)
- **83(b):** If vesting is subject to substantial risk of forfeiture, founders are typically advised to file an 83(b) election with the IRS within 30 days of grant. **[Attorney and tax advisor review required.]**

## 7. Option Pool

- Early-stage companies often reserve **10–20%** of post-money equity for an option pool (employees, advisors, future hires).
- Pool size and refresh should be agreed by founders and, after financing, by the board.
- **[Attorney review recommended]** for option plan and grant documents.
