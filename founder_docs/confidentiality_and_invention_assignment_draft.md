## 4. Confidentiality (Draft Clauses)

**4.1 Definition of Confidential Information.** “Confidential Information” means any non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of the Company, including without limitation: business plans, financials, customer and user data, product plans, code, algorithms, formulas, designs, marketing plans, and the existence and terms of this Agreement. It does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee; (b) was rightfully known to Employee without restriction before disclosure; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed by Employee without use of Company Confidential Information.

**4.2 Obligation.** Employee will not use or disclose Confidential Information except as necessary to perform duties for the Company or with Company’s prior written consent. Employee will use at least the same care as for Employee’s own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care.

**4.3 Return/Destroy.** Upon termination or at Company’s request, Employee will return or destroy all tangible Confidential Information and not retain copies. **[Attorney review:** Some jurisdictions or roles may require different treatment (e.g., legal hold). **]**

**4.4 Permitted Disclosure.** Employee may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, provided Employee gives Company prompt notice and cooperates in seeking protective order or similar relief. **[Attorney review:** Whistleblower and similar exceptions vary by state/federal law. **]**

---

## 5. Invention Assignment (Draft Clauses)

**5.1 Assignment.** Employee assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to all **Inventions** (as defined below) that: (a) are made, conceived, or reduced to practice during employment that relate to the Company’s business or actual or anticipated R&D; (b) result from work performed for the Company; or (c) are developed using Company resources or Confidential Information.

**5.2 Inventions.** “Inventions” means inventions, discoveries, ideas, works of authorship, software (source and object code), designs, formulas, processes, algorithms, data, know-how, and improvements, whether or not patentable or copyrightable.

**5.3 Prior Inventions / Exclusions.** **[Attorney review required:** California and other states require disclosure of prior inventions and limit assignment to those relating to Company business or developed using Company resources. Include a **Prior Inventions** schedule or attachment if required. **]**

**5.4 Moral Rights.** To the extent assignable, Employee assigns moral rights; where not assignable, Employee waives enforcement to the maximum extent permitted by law.

**5.5 Assistance.** Employee will assist the Company to secure and enforce rights in the Inventions (e.g., patent and copyright applications), at Company’s expense. If Company cannot secure Employee’s signature, Employee hereby designates Company as attorney-in-fact to execute documents on Employee’s behalf for that purpose.
