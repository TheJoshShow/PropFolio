## 4. Assignment of IP (Draft Clauses)

**4.1 Assignment.** Assignor hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all **Inventions** (as defined below) and all **Proprietary Rights** therein, whether or not patentable or registrable, that Assignor has made, conceived, or reduced to practice, alone or with others, (a) during the term of Assignor’s relationship with the Company that relate to the Company’s business or actual or anticipated research or development, or (b) that result from work performed by Assignor for the Company, or (c) that are developed using the Company’s resources or confidential information.

**4.2 Definition of Inventions.** “Inventions” means inventions, discoveries, ideas, works of authorship, mask works, designs, formulas, processes, algorithms, software (including source and object code), data, know-how, and improvements, whether or not patentable or subject to copyright or other intellectual property protection.

**4.3 Pre-Existing and Excluded IP.** The parties may attach a **Schedule A** listing (1) pre-existing IP that Assignor retains, and (2) IP excluded from assignment (e.g., prior inventions unrelated to the Company’s business). **[Attorney review required:** Exclusions and prior invention disclosure rules vary by state (e.g., California Labor Code). **]**

**4.4 Moral Rights.** To the extent assignable under applicable law, Assignor assigns any “moral rights” or similar rights. Where such rights cannot be assigned, Assignor waives enforcement to the maximum extent permitted by law.

**4.5 Further Assurances.** Assignor agrees to execute and deliver documents and take other actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect the Company’s ownership of the assigned IP (e.g., patent assignments, copyright registrations).

## 5. Representations and Warranties (Draft)

Assignor represents and warrants that: (a) Assignor has the right to grant the assignments in this Agreement; (b) no third party has any rights in the assigned IP that would conflict; (c) to Assignor’s knowledge, the assigned IP does not infringe any third party rights; and (d) Assignor has disclosed to the Company any prior inventions or IP that might be relevant (or listed them on Schedule A).
