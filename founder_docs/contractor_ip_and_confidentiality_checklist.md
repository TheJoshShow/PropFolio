# PropFolio — Contractor IP and Confidentiality Checklist

**Purpose:** Ensure every contractor engagement protects Company IP and confidential information.  
**Audience:** Founders, operations, legal  
**Date:** 2025-03-12

---

## 1. Before Engaging a Contractor

| # | Item | Done | Notes |
|---|------|------|--------|
| 1 | **Scope of work** defined in writing (SOW or statement of work) | ☐ | Deliverables, timeline, fees |
| 2 | **Contract type** chosen: fixed-price, T&M, or retainer | ☐ | Document in agreement |
| 3 | **IP ownership:** All work product and inventions related to the engagement assigned to Company | ☐ | Must be in contract; see Section 2 |
| 4 | **Confidentiality:** Contractor must keep Company info confidential and use only for the engagement | ☐ | NDA or clause in main agreement |
| 5 | **No conflict:** Contractor represents no other agreement conflicts and (if required) will not use third-party IP without permission | ☐ | Representation in agreement |
| 6 | **Background IP:** Contractor retains pre-existing IP; grant to Company limited license if needed for deliverables | ☐ | Define in contract |
| 7 | **Warranty:** Work is original, does not infringe third-party rights, and Contractor has right to assign | ☐ | Standard in work-for-hire / contractor agreements |
| 8 | **Indemnity:** Contractor indemnifies Company for breach of IP/confidentiality and third-party claims arising from Contractor’s work | ☐ | Scope and caps per risk; **[Attorney review recommended]** |
| 9 | **Term and termination:** Term, payment terms, effect of termination on IP (e.g., all work product delivered and assigned upon payment) | ☐ | |
| 10 | **Insurance:** If material risk, require contractor to carry appropriate insurance (e.g., E&O) | ☐ | Optional for low-risk engagements |

## 2. IP Assignment (Contractors)

- **Work made for hire:** In the U.S., under copyright law, “work made for hire” by an employee is owned by the employer. **Contractors** are not employees; therefore **contractor agreements must explicitly assign** all deliverables and inventions (or use work-for-hire language for copyrightable works that qualify under the Copyright Act).
- **Invention assignment:** Include assignment of all inventions, ideas, and improvements conceived or reduced to practice in the scope of the engagement. Broader than copyright “work for hire.”
- **Moral rights:** To the extent applicable, obtain waiver or assignment of moral rights.
- **Further assurances:** Contractor will sign documents to perfect Company’s rights (patent assignments, copyright registrations).

**[Attorney review recommended]** for all contractor agreements that involve code, design, or other IP.

---

## 3. Confidentiality

- **Definition:** Company confidential information (business, technical, user data, etc.) disclosed in the engagement.
- **Obligations:** Non-use except for the engagement; non-disclosure; return/destroy at end of engagement.
- **Exclusions:** Standard carve-outs (public, known, received from third party, independently developed) with attorney review.
- **Survival:** Obligation survives termination (e.g., 3–5 years or indefinite for trade secrets).

## 4. By Role (Quick Reference)

| Contractor type | IP assignment | NDA / confidentiality | Formal agreement |
|-----------------|---------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Developer (code)** | Required | Required | Written SOW + IP/NDA |
| **Designer (UI/UX, assets)** | Required | Required | Written SOW + IP/NDA |
| **Content / copy** | Required | Required | Written SOW + IP/NDA |
| **Consultant (strategy, no deliverables)** | Not always | Required | NDA + engagement letter |
| **Legal / accounting** | N/A (their work product may be theirs) | Required | Engagement letter (firm’s terms often apply) |
