# PropFolio — Founder Decision-Making and Deadlock Policy

**Purpose:** Define how material and day-to-day decisions are made and how founder deadlocks are resolved.  
**Audience:** Founders; counsel when drafting bylaws or shareholder/founder agreements  
**Date:** 2025-03-12

---

## 1. Principles

- **Clarity:** Everyone should know who can decide what and what happens when founders disagree.
- **Documentation:** Decision authority and deadlock resolution should be reflected in **bylaws**, **operating agreement**, or a **founder/shareholder agreement**.
- **Proportionality:** Day-to-day decisions can be delegated; material decisions typically require founder (or board) approval.

**[Attorney review recommended]** when encoding these rules in formal corporate documents. This memo is a policy framework only.

---

## 2. Decision Categories (Template)

| Category | Definition (example) | Authority (example) | Deadlock (if applicable) |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **Day-to-day operations** | Routine business within budget and strategy (e.g., vendor under $X, non-material product changes) | CEO for business; CTO for product/engineering | Escalate to material decision or designated tie-breaker |
| **Material contracts** | Commitments above $[X], multi-year, or involving exclusivity, IP, or key partnerships | Founder/board approval per threshold | Per Section 4 |
| **Hiring / termination** | Key hires (e.g., first 5–10 employees), termination of co-founders or key personnel | Unanimous or supermajority founder consent | Per Section 4 |
| **Equity / option grants** | New option pool, advisor equity, new founder or significant grant | Board + founder consent as required by cap table / bylaws | Per Section 4 |
| **Fundraising / dilution** | Priced round, SAFE/convertible note terms, material change in cap table | Unanimous founder consent (or per existing investor rights) | Per Section 4 |
| **Strategy / pivot** | Major product pivot, geography, or business model change | Unanimous founder consent | Per Section 4 |
| **IP / litigation** | Sale of IP, settlement of material dispute, initiation of material litigation | Unanimous or supermajority | Per Section 4 |
| **Corporate** | Formation, dissolution, merger, sale of company, amendment of charter/bylaws | Per corporate law and charter; typically board + shareholder vote | Per charter and state law |

*Customize thresholds (e.g., $X) and “unanimous” vs. “majority” with counsel.*

---

## 3. Tie-Breaker (Optional)

- For **50/50** or **even-split** founder teams, designate a **tie-breaker** for material decisions (e.g., CEO has final say on business matters; CTO on technical matters), or require **mediation** before any binding decision.
- Alternative: **third-party advisor or board observer** who can break ties on defined matters (document scope and selection process).

---

## 4. Deadlock Resolution (Draft Policy)

If founders cannot reach agreement on a **material decision** within a defined period (e.g., 15–30 days):

1. **Good-faith discussion:** One or more structured meetings to resolve.
2. **Mediation:** Non-binding mediation with a mutually agreed mediator (costs shared).
3. **Escalation:** If still deadlocked, either:
   - **Designated tie-breaker** (if adopted) makes the decision within scope, or
   - **Board** (if one exists with a non-founder member) decides, or
   - **Buy-sell / shotgun:** One founder offers to buy the other’s shares (or sell) at a formula price; other founder must accept one side. **[Attorney review required:** Shotgun and buy-sell provisions are complex and must be drafted by counsel. **]**
   - **Dissolution / exit:** As a last resort, follow state law and charter for deadlock dissolution (rare; avoid unless no other path).

**[Attorney review recommended]** for any buy-sell, shotgun, or drag-along/tag-along terms.

---

## 5. Documentation

- **Bylaws or operating agreement:** Specify voting thresholds for director and shareholder decisions.
- **Founder or shareholder agreement:** Specify consent requirements for material decisions, tie-breaker, and deadlock procedure.
- **Board consents:** Use written consents for formal board decisions and keep in corporate records ([corporate_records_checklist.md](corporate_records_checklist.md)).
