# PropFolio — Vesting Schedule Framework

**Purpose:** Outline vesting terms, cliffs, and acceleration for founder and employee equity.  
**Audience:** Founders; counsel when drafting option plans and stock agreements  
**Date:** 2025-03-12

---

## 1. Disclaimer

**[Attorney review recommended.]** Vesting has tax (e.g., 83(b)), securities, and employment implications. This document is a **framework** only. All vesting terms must be set forth in formal instruments (stock purchase agreements, option agreements, plan documents) prepared or reviewed by qualified U.S. counsel.

---

## 2. Why Vesting

- **Alignment:** Ties equity to continued contribution.
- **Investor expectation:** Standard in U.S. venture-backed companies.
- **Leaver treatment:** Defines what happens when a founder or key person leaves (unvested shares typically lapse or are repurchased).

---

## 3. Standard Parameters

| Term | Typical range | Common default | Notes |
|-----|----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Vesting period** | 3–4 years | 4 years | From grant or start date |
| **Cliff** | 0–12 months | 12 months | No vesting before cliff; at cliff, 25% (of 4-year) vests |
| **Vesting frequency after cliff** | Monthly or quarterly | Monthly | 1/36 of remainder per month (for 4-year with 1-year cliff) |
| **Double-trigger acceleration** | 0–100% | Often 0% at early stage | Vesting accelerates on change of control + termination (investor negotiation) |
| **Single-trigger acceleration** | Rare for founders | 0% | Not standard in VC-backed deals |

---

## 4. Founder Vesting

- **Recommendation:** Founders should be subject to the **same** vesting schedule (e.g., 4-year with 1-year cliff) as senior employees, unless investors or counsel advise otherwise.
- **Start date:** Vesting typically starts on earliest of: formation date, full-time start date, or date of agreement.
- **Repurchase:** Company (or assignee) typically has the right to repurchase unvested shares at cost (or fair market value, depending on structure) when founder leaves. **[Attorney review required for repurchase price and terms.]**
- **83(b) election:** If shares are subject to vesting and granted at fair market value (or low price), founders are usually advised to file an 83(b) election within **30 days** of grant. **[Tax advisor and attorney review required.]**

---

## 5. Acceleration (Change of Control)

- **Double-trigger:** Vesting accelerates only if (1) company is acquired (or similar change of control) **and** (2) participant is terminated without cause (or resigns for good reason) within a defined period. Common in VC term sheets.
- **Single-trigger:** Vesting accelerates on change of control alone. Rare for early-stage founders; sometimes negotiated for key executives in later rounds.
- Document acceleration in **option plan**, **stock agreement**, or **M&A** documents.
